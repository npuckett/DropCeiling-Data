## Abstract (139 words — under the 150 limit)

> DropCeiling turns a row of standard office light panels into a luminous body that shares
> a storefront with the bodies passing outside. Two cameras read pedestrians on the
> sidewalk, and an alcove in the architecture divides them into those who approach and
> those who pass. A single virtual point light moves through the panels in response,
> following, greeting, breathing, and settling, its position, brightness, and falloff the
> only output. This pictorial follows that exchange across three timescales: the instant of
> a gesture, the rhythm of an afternoon, and the slow drift of a personality over
> fifty-four days and a million encounters. Through annotated photographs and diagrams, we
> show how a system designed for interactions it could not anticipate composes its
> behaviour from presence alone, and how a whole street of movement is continually
> distilled into twelve values of light.

## Visual language (the craft the reviewers grade)

- **Two registers, consistently paired.** *Photographic* (the real bodies, the lit panels,
  the street) and *diagrammatic* (the greyscale A0/C-series system language). The recurring
  move of the whole pictorial is putting a photograph and its diagram **side by side** so
  the reader sees the lived moment and its mechanism at once.
- **Greyscale + one accent.** Keep the black/white/grey scheme already built (A0, C1-bw).
  Introduce a single warm accent reserved for *the light itself* (the point light and lit
  panels) so the eye always finds the luminous body on the page.
- **Time runs left→right / top→bottom.** Gestures, afternoons, and the 54-day arc all read
  as timelines so "the body in time" is legible as layout, not just content.
- **Annotation as voice.** Text lives as captions, callouts, and labels on images — not
  body paragraphs. The optional Intro/Conclusion are kept minimal (TEI allows omitting
  them).
- **Anonymization:** no author/institution/site names or recognisable faces in identifying
  ways for review; generalise the location ("a downtown storefront in a North American
  city"). Blur/again-consent any faces per ethics.

## Spread-by-spread storyboard (12 pages = ~6 spreads + title)

Each entry: the beat · the dominant image · supporting diagram · what the annotations say.

### P1 — Title + opening image (1 p)
- **Beat:** arrival. The luminous body in its doorway at night.
- **Image:** hero professional photo (P1 / `dcView`), full bleed.
- **Diagram:** none — let the photo carry it.
- **Annotation:** title, the 139-word abstract, 5 keywords. One line: *"a luminous body
  that shares a storefront with the bodies passing by."*

### Spread A (pp 2–3) — "The Stage": site, bodies, and the alcove
- **Beat:** the architecture sets the rules. The alcove divides approachers from passers.
- **Images:** wide street photo showing the column alcove + sidewalk traffic; an overhead
  or eye-level shot of someone stepping into the active zone.
- **Diagram:** **B3 spatial plan** (to-scale: panels, two cameras, active/passive zones),
  redrawn in the greyscale style and overlaid on / paired with the photo.
- **Annotation:** label active vs passive zone; "presence alone is the input — no screen,
  no instruction"; cameras "mounted inside, looking out."

### Spread B (pp 4–5) — "The Duet": the gesture vocabulary (the embodiment core)
- **Beat:** two bodies meet. Dwell deepens the exchange: notice → greet → engage → bond.
- **Images:** a **photo sequence** (strip) of one person dwelling, paired frame-for-frame
  with the light's response. This is the spread that most needs the professional stills.
- **Diagram:** the **16-gesture library as a notation plate** (nod, lean, sway, orbit,
  settle, breathe, bloom, sweep, focus…), each as a small motion glyph; the **dwell-phase
  timeline** drawn like a score (0-3 s / 3-10 s / 10-30 s / 30 s+).
- **Annotation:** name each gesture; mark where on the dwell score the photo frames sit.
- **NEW ASSET:** gesture-notation plate; the paired photo/response strip.

### Spread C (pp 6–7) — "The Collapse": many bodies → twelve lights (the foldout)
- **Beat:** the central idea — a street of movement distilled, 30×/s, to twelve values.
- **Image:** the 12 panels lit (photo) at one edge; real pedestrians at the other.
- **Diagram:** **A0 master overview** *or* the **C1 funnel** as the spread's spine
  (greyscale, already built and on-style). Optionally **C3 per-panel falloff** as an inset.
- **Annotation:** "≈9-value point-light state → 12 DMX bytes"; "each panel brightens by
  distance." This is the systems-collapse argument, made visual.

### Spread D (pp 8–9) — "Three Timescales": instant, afternoon, season
- **Beat:** the body in time. Reaction (instant) ⊂ anticipation (afternoon) ⊂ self-tuning
  (days).
- **Images:** a 24-hour rhythm strip built from real data (quiet night → commute → lunch).
- **Diagram:** **A2 / A0 nested loops**; **A5 trend windows**; **D2 fast-vs-slow cadence**.
- **Annotation:** timescales labelled; "the outer loops never move the light — they change
  the rules the inner loop plays by."
- **NEW ASSET:** 24-hour rhythm strip from the database.

### Spread E (pp 10–11) — "The Light That Learned": 54 days, friction, drift
- **Beat:** designing for the unanticipated; how it stayed alive without going stale.
- **Images:** before/after — week-1 vs week-7 behaviour.
- **Diagram:** **personality radar (neutral vs as-deployed)** — *NEW, long-promised*;
  **A7 self-tuning loop** (greyscale); **D1 run totals**; the **interaction-budget** sketch
  (spend/refill/throttle).
- **Annotation:** "~1M encounters, 54 days"; "friction kept it from turning every slider to
  maximum"; the measurable changes (more confident, less repetitive).
- **NEW ASSET:** personality radar; week-1-vs-week-7 comparison from the database.

### P12 / back — "Made by a model of itself" + close (1 p)
- **Beat:** brief method note — the spatial 3D model as the shared brain for human + AI
  authoring; the pygame twin.
- **Images:** the pygame 3D twin (P4) beside the real install (P1) — same coordinate frame.
- **Diagram:** small authoring-loop sketch (the proposed **E1**), optional.
- **Annotation:** 2–3 sentences; then references (excluded from the 12-page count).
